History

Kedarnath is a pilgrimage site dedicated to the Hindu deity Shiva. It is one of the four pilgrimage spots of the Uttarkhand Char Dham yatra. The temple's construction is credited to the Pandava brothers from the Mahabharata, though earlier references to Kedarnath are found in the Skanda Purana.

According to some accounts, Adi Shankaracharya died near Kedarnath, and the monument marking his resting place still exists here. Kedarnath was a prominent pilgrimage centre by the 12th century, mentioned in Kritya-kalpataru.

Demographics

As of the 2011 India census, Kedarnath has a population of 830. Males constitute 99% of the population, while females make up 1%. The average literacy rate is 63%, with male literacy at 63% and female literacy at 36%. The town sees a significant rise in population due to pilgrim influx, reaching up to 10 lakh visitors in 2022.

Climate

The temple is closed during the winter months due to heavy snowfall. From November to April, the palanquin with the Utsava Murti of Kedarnath is moved to Ukhimath. The town experiences a monsoon-influenced subarctic climate, with cold winters and mild, rainy summers.

Places of Interest

Other than the Kedarnath Temple, places of interest include the Bhairavnath Temple, Chorabari Tal (Gandhi Sarovar), Kedarnath Wildlife Sanctuary, and the Adi Shankaracharya Samadhi.
